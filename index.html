How to read this

What everything means

EdgeBoard turns a lot of data into one number per bet. Here is what that number is, how it is built, and what every term on the board and in the analyzer actually means. Nothing here is betting advice; it is a key to the tool.

The rating Letter grades Rating inputs Board terms Value signals Tracking terms Odds basics

The rating: the one number that matters

Every prop gets a single signed percentage, shown for whichever side is stronger. It answers one question: at the best price available, do you have an edge, and how big?

B+ · O +3.2%

Reads as: the Over is the better side, it earns a B+ grade, and the model thinks it wins 3.2 percentage points more often than the price implies. Green means the edge is yours.

D · U −2.1%

Reads as: the Under is the less-bad side but still negative. The book has a 2.1-point edge on you here. Red means pass.

The percentage is the model's win probability minus the price's implied probability. A rating past ±15% almost always means stale or mismatched data, so it caps and flags a warning rather than pretending it is real.

Letter grades

The grade is just the rating translated to a report card, so you can scan fast without reading decimals. Bands:

A+ 6%+ edge
A 4.5–6%
A- 3.5–4.5%
B+ 2.5–3.5%
B 1.5–2.5%
B- 0.75–1.5%
C roughly even
D −0.75 to −3%
F worse than −3%

A grade is a screen, not a lock. Even an A+ loses plenty of the time; it just wins often enough that the price is wrong in your favor. The bet log's grade-accuracy table is what proves whether the grades actually hold up over time.

What feeds the rating

Tap any grade to open its breakdown. These are the pieces it blends:

Model projection
EdgeBoard's own predicted number for the stat, from a weighted recent-form and season average, adjusted for the matchup. The "Model over %" is the chance the result clears the line given that projection and the player's game-to-game volatility.
History
How often the player actually cleared this exact line in past games, weighted toward recent form. The most literal signal: did they do it before?
Market fair %
The book's own opinion with the juice stripped out. The blend is pulled toward this based on sample size, because the market is usually sharp and thin samples should defer to it.
Blend
55% model, 45% history, then shrunk toward the market fair %. The market always keeps at least 40% of the say. This blended probability is what the rating is measured against the price.
Usage
From nflverse: whether the player's recent targets, carries, or pass attempts are trending above or below their season norm. Rising usage nudges the projection up, capped at ±8%.
Defense
How much the opponent allows in this exact stat versus league average, capped at ±10%. "Stat-specific" means real yardage/receptions allowed; "pts proxy" is the fallback when detailed data is missing.
Script
Game script from the market's spread and total. Big underdogs throw more, big favorites run more, high totals add volume. Only applies when team props are loaded for the game.
Weather
Wind, precipitation, and hard cold trim passing and receiving projections for outdoor games. Domes are neutral.
Consistency
How tight the player's game-to-game range is. A steady producer is a safer edge than a boom-or-bust one at the same rating.

On the board

Consensus line
The median line across every book posting the prop. The market's agreed-upon number.
Best over / best under
The most favorable price on each side across all books, with which book has it. Always betting the best available price is free edge.
No-vig over %
The market's true probability of the over after removing the bookmaker's built-in margin.
Confidence
High, Medium, or Low, based on how many games of data and how many books back the rating. Low means treat it lightly.
Drift
Books disagree on the line by a meaningful amount. Where books are slow to move is where soft numbers hide.
Steam
The line moved sharply since you first loaded it, with an arrow for direction. Often a sign of sharp money.
Top plays
A ranked leaderboard of the best-rated plays across every loaded game and market at once.
The ticker
The scrolling strip up top streams your highest edges once games are scored.

Value signals

ARB Arbitrage
The best over and best under across different books lock in a guaranteed profit no matter the outcome. Rare and usually small, but risk-free when real.
MID Middle
You can bet the over at a low line and the under at a higher one elsewhere; if the result lands between, both win. If not, you lose only the juice.
Lowest juice
Which book charges the least margin on this prop. Paying 4.5% hold instead of 8% keeps more of every bet over time.
Alt-line ladder
The same prop priced at every alternate line the book offers, each graded, so you can find which rung is the real value.
Kelly stake
A recommended bet size as a share of your bankroll, based on your edge. EdgeBoard uses quarter-Kelly, a conservative fraction that protects against the model being slightly wrong.

Tracking your results

CLV (closing line value)
Whether you got a better number than the line closed at. Consistently beating the close is the earliest, most reliable sign you have a real edge, long before your win rate means anything.
EV (expected value)
Average profit per $100 staked if the model's probability is right. Positive EV is the whole goal.
ROI
Total profit divided by total staked across settled bets.
Grade accuracy
How each grade band has actually performed in your settled bets. This is the honest scoreboard: it tells you whether an "A" really wins like an A.
Calibration
Whether the model's 60% bets actually win about 60% of the time. A well-calibrated model can be trusted; a miscalibrated one is over- or under-confident.
Auto-settle
Pulls final box scores to mark your logged bets win, loss, or push automatically.

Odds basics

American odds
A minus number (−110) is how much you stake to win $100; a plus number (+150) is how much you win on a $100 stake.
Implied probability
The win chance baked into a price. −110 implies about 52.4%. That 52.4% is also the breakeven: win more than that and you profit long-term.
The vig (juice / hold)
The book's built-in margin. It is why both sides of a coin flip are priced at −110 instead of +100, and why beating the market is hard.
Over / Under
Betting whether the real result finishes above or below the posted line.
Push
The result lands exactly on the line; the bet refunds.

Launch checklist

Work through these once after deploying. They verify the data pipe before you lean on it.

    How the model works

    Consensus and drift. The consensus is the median line across books. A book sitting well off consensus gets flagged; that is where line-shopping edges live.

    No-vig probability. Each book's over and under prices are converted to implied probabilities and the juice is stripped, giving the market's true opinion of the over.

    Projection. 60% weighted recent form (last five games, newest weighted heaviest) blended with 40% season average, then adjusted for the opponent and the weather. Over probability comes from the player's own game-to-game volatility.

    Defense adjustment. Uses points allowed per game from ESPN standings as a defense strength proxy, capped at ±8%. It is a proxy, not position-specific yardage allowed, and is labeled as such wherever it is used.

    Weather adjustment. Outdoor games only. Wind of 15+ mph trims passing and receiving projections 5%, 20+ mph trims 10%, and a wet forecast trims another 3%. Domes and closed roofs are neutral.

    Edge, EV and CLV. Edge is model or historical probability minus the price's implied probability. EV is expected profit per $100 if that probability holds. CLV compares your line to the closing line; beating the close is the earliest sign of a real edge.

    Futures. Team outrights come off the same odds feed. The no-vig market % normalizes every book's full field to 100%, and a value flag means the best available price beats that consensus. Player award futures are not on the free feed, so the futures tab includes a calculator that strips the vig from any list you paste from a book.

    The rating. Each prop's signed percentage is the blend's probability minus the implied probability of the best available price, shown for whichever side is stronger. The blend is 55% model projection and 45% historical hit rate, then shrunk toward the market's no-vig opinion based on sample size; the market always keeps at least 40% of the say, and thin samples lean on it harder. Green positive means edge to you at that price, red negative means the book has you. Ratings past ±15% almost always mean stale or mismatched data, so verify before trusting one. Team prop ratings are market-based only: fair probability against the best price, with no player model behind them.

    Usage data. Player ratings also fold in an nflverse usage trend: a player whose recent targets, carries, or attempts run above their season norm gets a small projection boost, capped at ±8%. If the usage file fails to load, ratings still work without it.

    Injuries. Official ESPN injury designations gate every rating. Out, doubtful, IR, PUP or suspended players show a red status pill instead of a rating, since a prop on an unavailable player is dead money. Questionable players keep their rating but are capped at low confidence and tagged with a Q.

    Defense, upgraded. When usage data is loaded, the defense adjustment is stat-specific: passing yards allowed for passing props, receiving yards or receptions allowed for receiving props, and so on, built from every player's production against that defense, capped at ±10%. Without it, the points-allowed proxy still applies. The detail row shows which source was used.

    Game script and steam. If team props are loaded for a game, the market's own spread and total nudge player projections: big underdogs lean pass, big favorites lean run, and high totals add volume, capped at ±8% combined. Lines that move meaningfully between your pulls get a steam badge showing direction and size.

    Honest roadmap: not built, and why

    Public betting percentages. Bet-vs-money splits and reverse line movement need paid feeds (Action Network tier). The steam badges are the free stand-in.

    Live in-game props. Needs real-time infrastructure and burns credits fast. Out of scope for a free static build.

    Referee tendencies and man/zone matchup charting. No reliable free structured source. The stat-specific defense splits are the free approximation.

    True closing-line capture. The sync button fills closes from whatever board you have loaded. Fully automatic capture at kickoff needs a small Vercel cron job; say the word when you want that built.

    Data and caches

    Gamelogs and defense data cache in this browser for 12 hours to keep things fast and stay polite to the free endpoints. Line snapshots persist so the board can show movement.
