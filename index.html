    How the model works

    Consensus and drift. The consensus is the median line across books. A book sitting well off consensus gets flagged; that is where line-shopping edges live.

    No-vig probability. Each book's over and under prices are converted to implied probabilities and the juice is stripped, giving the market's true opinion of the over.

    Projection. 60% weighted recent form (last five games, newest weighted heaviest) blended with 40% season average, then adjusted for the opponent and the weather. Over probability comes from the player's own game-to-game volatility.

    Defense adjustment. Uses points allowed per game from ESPN standings as a defense strength proxy, capped at ±8%. It is a proxy, not position-specific yardage allowed, and is labeled as such wherever it is used.

    Weather adjustment. Outdoor games only. Wind of 15+ mph trims passing and receiving projections 5%, 20+ mph trims 10%, and a wet forecast trims another 3%. Domes and closed roofs are neutral.

    Edge, EV and CLV. Edge is model or historical probability minus the price's implied probability. EV is expected profit per $100 if that probability holds. CLV compares your line to the closing line; beating the close is the earliest sign of a real edge.

    Futures. Team outrights come off the same odds feed. The no-vig market % normalizes every book's full field to 100%, and a value flag means the best available price beats that consensus. Player award futures are not on the free feed, so the futures tab includes a calculator that strips the vig from any list you paste from a book.

    Data and caches

    Gamelogs and defense data cache in this browser for 12 hours to keep things fast and stay polite to the free endpoints. Line snapshots persist so the board can show movement.
